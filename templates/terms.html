02
Service Description

KeyVora is a server-assisted encrypted password manager. The service allows you to:

  • Store credentials (titles, usernames, passwords, URLs, notes) in encrypted notebooks
  • Organise entries into named, colour-coded notebooks
  • Access credentials from any browser after authenticating with your master password
  • Enable TOTP two-factor authentication for additional login security
  • Export your vault data as a JSON file at any time

KeyVora is provided free of charge with no trial periods, feature limits, or subscription tiers. The service is offered on an "as-is" and "as-available" basis.

KeyVora uses a server-assisted encryption model, not a fully client-side zero-knowledge architecture. Your vault key is held in the server session while the vault is unlocked and wiped automatically after 15 minutes of inactivity.

04
Master Password & Security

Your master password is the key that protects your vault. It is used to derive a Key Encryption Key (KEK) via PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 with 390,000 iterations. The master password is never stored on our servers in any form.

If you forget your master password, we cannot view or recover it. Recovery of access depends on the account recovery mechanisms available at the time and may require verification through your registered email address. Always store your master password securely outside of KeyVora.

A password reset flow is available, but it re-wraps your existing vault key under the new master password — this only works if you can authenticate via your registered email address.

We strongly recommend enabling TOTP two-factor authentication after registration. Backup codes are generated at enrolment and should be stored securely offline; they are single-use and cannot be regenerated.

06
Data & Privacy

We collect only the data necessary to provide the service: your username, email address, bcrypt-hashed login password, encrypted vault key (wrapped by your KEK), and Fernet-encrypted credential entries.

What we store

  • Email address — used for authentication, password reset OTPs, and service notifications
  • Username — used for display purposes within the application
  • Bcrypt-hashed login password — stored as a one-way hash; never reversible
  • Encrypted vault key — wrapped by your KEK; useless without your master password
  • Encrypted credential entries — stored as Fernet ciphertext; readable only with the vault key
  • TOTP secret and bcrypt-hashed backup codes — only if 2FA is enabled

We do not sell, trade, or share your data with any third party for marketing or commercial purposes. Your encrypted data may be used in aggregated, anonymised form to monitor service health.

You may export your complete vault at any time via the dashboard and request account deletion, which permanently removes all your data from our systems.

07
Security Practices

KeyVora implements the following security measures on your behalf:

  • Fernet (AES-128-CBC + HMAC-SHA256) encryption for all stored credentials
  • PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 with 390,000 iterations for master password key derivation
  • Bcrypt hashing for login passwords and 2FA backup codes
  • Rate-limited endpoints to prevent brute-force attacks
  • Auto-lock vault after 15 minutes of inactivity, wiping the vault key from session memory
  • Security response headers (CSP, X-Frame-Options, X-Content-Type-Options, Referrer-Policy) on all responses
  • HttpOnly, SameSite=Lax, and Secure session cookies in production

While we implement industry-standard security controls, we cannot guarantee absolute security. You accept responsibility for your own security hygiene: use a strong, unique master password, enable 2FA, and do not share your credentials.
